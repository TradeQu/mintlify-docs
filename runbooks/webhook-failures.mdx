## What fired

A high share of webhook delivery attempts are failing.

| Alert | Condition | For |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `MeshQuWebhookDeliveryFailures` (warning) | failed deliveries > **10%** of all attempts | 5 min |

## Symptoms

- Tenants report missing or delayed alert webhooks.
- Failure-rate panel for webhook deliveries is above the threshold line.
- A retry-driven backlog may build in parallel — see [webhook-backlog](/runbooks/webhook-backlog).

## First actions

1. **Scope by destination.** Is the failure concentrated on one tenant endpoint, or broad across many? A single unreachable endpoint can dominate the ratio.
2. **Identify the failure class.** Connection refused / DNS / TLS failures point at the destination; 4xx/5xx from the destination point at the receiving application; timeouts point at either side under load.
3. **Check egress health.** If failures are broad across many destinations, suspect a MeshQu-side egress or network problem rather than individual endpoints.
4. **Confirm retries are flowing.** Failures are retried with backoff; confirm the worker is alive and retrying rather than silently dropping.
5. **Decide notification.** If a specific tenant's endpoint is persistently down, the tenant may need to be notified to fix their receiver.

<Note>
A webhook failure delays a notification — it never affects the durability or verifiability of a recorded decision. Decisions and receipts remain intact and independently verifiable regardless of webhook outcome.
</Note>
