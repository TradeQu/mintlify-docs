## What fired

A high proportion of decisions are returning `DENY`.

| Alert | Condition | For |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `MeshQuHighDenyRate` (warning) | `DENY` verdicts > **30%** of all decisions over 1h | 15 min |

This is a **business-logic** signal, not an infrastructure fault. The API is healthy; it is the *outcome distribution* that is unusual.

## Symptoms

- The deny-rate panel is above the threshold line.
- A tenant may report that more of their traffic is being denied than expected.

## First actions

1. **This is not an outage.** A high deny rate means policies are denying more decisions — by design, MeshQu is advisory, so it is reporting verdicts, not blocking traffic itself.
2. **Scope it.** Is the spike on one tenant, one policy, or one decision type? A single policy change is the most common cause.
3. **Correlate with policy changes.** A newly published or activated policy version can shift the deny rate sharply. Check whether the onset lines up with a policy lifecycle event.
4. **Distinguish intended from regression.** A stricter policy *should* deny more — that may be correct. A misconfigured rule denying legitimate traffic is a problem. Confirm with the policy owner / tenant before acting.
5. **Do not change policy reactively.** Policy is the tenant's governed control. Investigate and notify; do not silently alter rules to clear an alert.

<Note>
MeshQu is **advisory by design**. A `DENY` verdict is a recommendation the tenant's own system acts on — MeshQu does not block anything. A high deny rate is a signal about *policy outcomes*, not a MeshQu malfunction.
</Note>
