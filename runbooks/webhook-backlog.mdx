## What fired

Pending webhook deliveries are accumulating faster than they drain.

| Alert | Condition | For |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `MeshQuWebhookDeliveryBacklog` (warning) | > **100** pending deliveries in a 10-minute window | 10 min |

## Symptoms

- Tenants report delayed alert webhooks.
- The pending-deliveries panel is climbing rather than draining.
- Webhook latency (enqueue → delivered) is rising.

## First actions

1. **Confirm direction.** Is the backlog still growing, plateaued, or draining? A transient spike that is already draining may need no action.
2. **Check delivery health.** A backlog often pairs with elevated failures — cross-check the [webhook-failures](/runbooks/webhook-failures) signal. Failing endpoints get retried, which inflates the queue.
3. **Check the worker path.** Confirm the delivery worker is running and processing. A stalled worker is the classic cause of a monotonically growing backlog.
4. **Scope by destination.** One slow or unreachable tenant endpoint can dominate the queue. Identify whether the backlog is concentrated on a single destination.
5. **Check resource pressure.** Cross-reference [high-memory](/runbooks/high-memory) and [event-loop-lag](/runbooks/event-loop-lag) — resource starvation slows delivery throughput.

<Note>
Webhooks are an at-least-once notification channel, not a system of record. A delivery backlog delays notifications; it does not lose or alter any recorded decision. Decisions and receipts remain durable and independently verifiable regardless of webhook state.
</Note>
