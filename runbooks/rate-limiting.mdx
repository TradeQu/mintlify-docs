## What fired

Rate limiting is rejecting requests at an elevated rate.

| Alert | Condition | For |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `MeshQuHighRateLimitHits` (info) | rate-limit rejections > **10/sec** | 5 min |

This is an **informational** signal — rate limiting working as designed is not an outage. It is worth a look because it can indicate a misbehaving client, a retry storm, or abuse.

## Symptoms

- A client reports `429 Too Many Requests` responses.
- The rate-limit-hits panel is above the threshold line.

## First actions

1. **Identify the source.** Is the rejection concentrated on one tenant or IP, or broad? A single client in a retry loop is the common cause.
2. **Decide: legitimate or abusive.** A real traffic increase from a growing tenant may warrant a limit adjustment; a retry storm or scraping pattern warrants leaving the limit in place.
3. **Check for cascading retries.** Clients that retry aggressively on `429` make the situation worse. The fix is often on the client side (backoff).
4. **Adjust only deliberately.** Rate limits are a protective control. Raising them is a deliberate, scoped decision — not a reflex to clear an alert.

<Note>
Rate limiting protects the API from overload and abuse. Rejected requests are not lost decisions — the client receives a `429` and should retry with backoff. No recorded decision is affected.
</Note>
